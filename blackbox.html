My BlackBox Journey

What I did

During the BlackBox Project, I worked to investigate what was inside a sealed box without opening it, it had tubes and mechanisms that would when you poured water the water would pour out in the end weirdly. We recorded our observations and created physical models on what we think is inside of the BlackBox. After that, we went on AutoDesk Maya to make a digital model on what we think is in the box, but we thought thoroughly about it and made the model, we then finally made it an animation.

What I Learned

Through this project, I learned how to use observations and evidence to make conclusions about things I cannot directly see. I learned that guessing is almost never correct. Instead, I gathered evidence, make predictions, test ideas, and rechecked my data based on new information. The Black Box project helped me understand the importance of using evidence to support a claim and showed me how scientific models can be used to explain observations.

21st Century Skill

The 21st-century skill where I showed the most growth was critical thinking. During the project, I had to analyze observations, evaluate different possibilities, and decide which explanation was best supported by the evidence. I learned how to think carefully about the information I collected instead of making assumptions. This project helped me improve my problem-solving skills and taught me the importance of using logic and evidence when making decisions.
