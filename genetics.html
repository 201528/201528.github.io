My Genetic Project Journey

What I did

During the genetics project, I completed several activities that helped me understand how DNA testing works. First, we watched videos and learned about genetics and how traits are passed down through DNA. Then, we collected our own DNA samples by swabbing the inside of our mouths. After collecting the samples, we mixed them with different liquids to prepare them for testing. The samples were then placed into a machine that analyzed our DNA. Before getting our results, I did an interview where I made predictions about what I thought the test would show. Once the results were ready, I completed a final interview where I reacted to the results and compared them to my original predictions.

What I learned

Through this project, I learned a lot about DNA and how scientists study genetics. I learned that DNA contains genetic information that can influence certain traits and characteristics. I also learned how DNA samples are collected, prepared, and analyzed using scientific equipment. One of the most interesting parts of the project was seeing how the actual results compared to my predictions. This helped me understand the importance of using evidence and data instead of making assumptions. Overall, the project gave me a better understanding of genetics and showed me how science can be used to learn more about ourselves and the world around us.

21st Century

The 21st-century skill where I showed the most growth was communication. Throughout the project, I had to explain my ideas during both the prediction interview and the final interview. I shared my thoughts, discussed my results, and explained what I learned from the data. I also had to listen carefully to information from the videos and class discussions so that I could better understand the project. This experience helped me become more confident speaking about expressing my ideas clearly.
